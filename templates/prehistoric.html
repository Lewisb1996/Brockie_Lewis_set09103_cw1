Big Cats of the Past

Long before current time, there were big cats that lived in prehtoric times alongside other prehistoric mammals like Woolly Mammoths and early humans. They have of course disappeared into extinction, but the discovery of fossils has enabled us to learn about them.

Here are some of the most well known prehistoric big cats that once roamed this planet.

Smilodon

Smilodon was 1 of the most famous prehistoric mammals and the best known of the "Sabre-Tooth Cats".

They lived in both North and South America during the Pleistocene Era (10,000 years ago) and were believed to have lived in "Prides" like modern day Lions.

Like other Sabre-Tooth Cats, Smilodon was equipped with 2 long slender canine teeth that with 1 single bite, would kill their prey.

Smilodon preyed on large herbivores including Mammoths, Macrauchenia, Toxodon, Bison, Camelops (Giant Camels) and Giant Ground Sloths.

They became extinct due to the large herbivores they preyed on vanishing due to climate change, as well as comeptiton with early Humans.

American Lion

American Lions were gigantic compared to modern day Lions, scientists believe they were 25% larger.

They lives in Prides in North America during the Pleistocene Era and were bleieved to be 1 of the top predator's as it was bigger then Smilodon.

Their favourite prey appeared to be Bison along with ealry Horses, Cameelops, large Deer and Mammoths. It was heavily involved in competition with the giant Short-Faced Bear (larger than modern day Polar Bears).

American Lions became extinct along woith Mammoths and other Ice Age mammals as early humans arrived and took their place as the top predator.

Dinofelis

Dinofelis were similar to modern day Leopard (although much larger) and were widespread across Africa, Asia and Europe

They were solitary cats like Leopards and lived during the Pliocene and Pleistocene Eras in all sorts of habitats

Its believed by scientists that Dinofelis was the main predator of Australopithecus (1 of our earliest ansestors) and also preyed on early Baboons and Antelope.

Its thought that Dinofelis was driven to extinction by climate change at the start of the Ice Age.
